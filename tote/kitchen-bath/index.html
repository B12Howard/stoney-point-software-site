What the selection process looks like without Tote.

You finalize selections for cabinetry, hardware, tile, fixtures, countertops, appliances. You send a PDF or a spreadsheet. The client opens it, doesn't know where to start, and sets it aside. When they do respond, it's partial — approved on some items, still deciding on others, asking to see one of the options again.

You're now tracking approval status in your head, managing revisions across email threads, and chasing a sign-off and a check separately. Meanwhile your contractor's schedule is moving on without a confirmed order.

What changes with Tote.

Tote is the client-facing portal on top of whatever you already use. You don't have to switch your design or rendering software.

  • Each product presented with images, specs, and pricing, in a format built for a decision
  • Clients respond item by item — approve, request a change, or ask a question
  • A revision goes out as a new Tote, so the client's always looking at the current version
  • Sign-off and payment happen in the same session, right when the client approves
  • Confirmed selections sync to your workflow, ready to turn into a purchase order

Who it's built for.

  • Kitchen and bath design firms currently using PDF exports or spreadsheets for client presentations
  • Designers using design/rendering software who want a better client-facing layer on top
  • Studios where presentation and purchase order are weeks apart
  • Teams creating purchase orders by hand from scattered email approvals
