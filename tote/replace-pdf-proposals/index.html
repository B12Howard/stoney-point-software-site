The PDF was designed for printing, not for decisions.

A PDF presents everything at once. It doesn't guide the client through a decision — it hands them a document and asks them to figure out what to do with it. They skim. They save it for later. They open it on their phone and can't read the specs. They forward it to their partner and don't follow up.

When a response does come back, it covers a dozen items in one email, out of order, with no clear approval on anything specific. You're now reconstructing a decision from a thread.

What Tote does instead.

  • One link, not a file. Opens on any device, no download, no account.
  • Each item presented individually. Clients respond to one thing at a time — approve, request a change, ask a question. Responses come back organized.
  • Approval is explicit and recorded. No ambiguity about what was confirmed.
  • Payment happens in the same session as approval — not three days later.
  • Confirmed items move into your fulfillment workflow automatically.
