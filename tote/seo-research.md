# Tote SEO Research — July 2026

## Competitor Map

| Industry | Incumbent Tools | Key Weakness (Tote's opening) |
|---|---|---|
| Interior Design | Mydoma, Studio Designer, Programa, DesignFiles, HoneyBook, Planify, Fohlio | Heavy PM suites ($64–$109/user/mo); designers want a lightweight client-facing layer, not a full ERP |
| Kitchen & Bath | Houzz Pro, 2020 Design, Cyncly Design Flex | Focused on 3D rendering/cabinet layouts; approval/payment workflow is an afterthought |
| Event Planning | HoneyBook, Rock Paper Coin, Planning Pod, Releventful, Plutio | Proposal + contract tools, but fragmented from payment and procurement; approval loop is email |
| Travel Advisors | Travefy, TravelJoy, AXUS, WeTravel | Itinerary builders with light payment; no curated "shoppable" selection experience |
| Commercial Furniture / AV | Proposify, PandaDoc, Qwilr, Better Proposals, DealHub | Generic proposal builders; no industry-specific approval-to-procurement flow |
| Corporate Gifting | Sendoso, SwagUp, Imprint Engine | Platform sells the gifts; no tool for curators/gifting agencies presenting curated sets to corporate clients |

## Strategic Notes

- **Tote's whitespace**: Competitors own the full PM suite (Mydoma, Studio Designer at $70–$109/user/mo) or the generic proposal tool (Qwilr, Proposify). No one owns "lightweight, client-facing, industry-specific presentation + approval + payment."
- **Lowest-hanging fruit**: "FF&E approval software", "interior design client approval software", "kitchen bath designer client portal" — all low difficulty, high commercial intent, essentially uncontested.
- **Quick win comparison**: Mydoma and Studio Designer are now the same company. Designers are actively looking for lighter alternatives.
- **Build topical authority in this order**: Interior Design → Problem-based cross-industry → Event Planning → Travel Advisors → Commercial Furniture/AV
